# Long-Term Care Industry: Trends & Outlook Report (2025–2026)

*Prepared: March 2026*

---

## Executive Summary

The long-term care (LTC) industry is undergoing a period of significant transformation driven by an aging population, evolving consumer preferences, technological innovation, regulatory shifts, and persistent workforce challenges. The global LTC market is valued at approximately $1.24 trillion in 2025 and is projected to reach $2.31 trillion by 2035 (CAGR of 6.42%). In the U.S. alone, the market is valued at $390.6 billion and expected to nearly double to $741 billion by 2034.

---

## 1. Demographic Drivers

- **Rapid aging population:** The U.S. population aged 65+ is projected to reach 73 million by 2030, with over 11,000 Americans turning 65 every day. By 2050, nearly a quarter of all Americans will be 65 or older.
- **Fastest-growing segment:** The 85+ age group — which requires the most intensive care — is growing even faster than the broader senior population.
- **Rising chronic conditions:** Approximately 93% of adults 65+ live with at least one chronic condition, and nearly 79% live with two or more.
- **Care need is near-universal:** 56% of Americans turning 65 today will need some level of long-term care services in their lifetime, yet only about 3% of Americans over 50 have any LTC insurance protection.

---

## 2. The Shift to Home-Based Care

Home-based care has emerged as the dominant growth area in the LTC sector:

- **Strong consumer preference:** Nearly 90% of seniors want to age in place. A September 2025 Pew Research survey found that 60% of older adults who live independently would prefer to stay home with a caregiver rather than move to a facility.
- **Market size:** The U.S. home care industry is projected to generate over $107 billion in revenue in 2025, with CMS projecting home healthcare spending to increase 7.1% between 2025 and 2026.
- **Fastest-growing occupation:** Home health and personal care aides are projected to grow 21% over the next decade, making it one of the fastest-growing occupations in the country.
- **Policy tailwinds:** Medicare broadened coverage for home health services in 2026 with improved access and reduced barriers. Over 30 states now provide Medicaid waiver programs to fund home-based alternatives to nursing care.
- **HCBS expansion:** Families are prioritizing transparency, care continuity, and affordability, and providers increasingly recognize that building-centered models alone no longer meet the realities of an aging population.

---

## 3. Workforce Crisis

The LTC workforce remains the industry's most pressing challenge:

### Current State
- Nursing homes have lost nearly **229,000 caregivers (14%+ of the workforce)** since February 2020 — the worst job loss of any healthcare sector.
- More than **60% of nursing homes** have limited new patient admissions due to staffing shortages.
- Average nursing care hours declined 7%, from 4.13 to 3.85 hours per resident per day between July 2015 and July 2025, driven by a 19% drop in RN hours.

### Projected Shortages
- The projected nursing supply in 2026 will meet only **91.94% of demand**, leaving a shortage rate of 8.06%.
- Licensed practical nurse (LPN) shortages are the most severe at 20% and are projected to double to 28% over the next decade.
- A projected shortfall of **300,000+ full-time LPN roles** by the mid-2030s if current trends persist.
- **Rural vs. urban divide:** Non-metro areas face a projected 14% shortage over the next decade vs. just 2% in metro areas.

### Positive Signs
- Turnover is declining: CNA turnover dropped from 44.16% to 42.34%; top-level executive turnover fell from 31.97% to 22.12% (per the 2025–2026 AHCANCAL Nursing Home Salary & Benefits Report).

---

## 4. Regulatory Landscape

### Repeal of Federal Minimum Staffing Standards
The most significant regulatory development was the full repeal of the Biden-era minimum staffing mandate:

- **April 2024:** CMS finalized a rule requiring 3.48 hours per resident day (HPRD) of total nurse staffing, including 0.55 HPRD of RN care and 2.45 HPRD of nurse aide care, plus 24/7 RN presence.
- **May–October 2024:** Federal courts in Texas and Iowa vacated the rule, finding it exceeded CMS's statutory authority.
- **July 2025:** Congress enacted Public Law 119-21, prohibiting CMS from implementing the staffing standards until September 30, 2034.
- **December 2025:** CMS formally repealed the staffing requirements via interim final rule (effective February 2, 2026).
- **Estimated impact:** CMS projects average annual savings of ~$2.18 billion and ~$21.8 billion over 10 years from the repeal.
- **Controversy:** Advocates argue the rule would have saved an estimated 13,000 lives annually; the industry argued it was infeasible given workforce shortages.

### Other Regulatory Developments
- **TEAM Model (2026):** CMS's Transforming Episode Accountability in Medicare model shifts hospital accountability for preventable readmissions from downstream SNFs, potentially reshaping hospital-SNF referral patterns.
- **State-level variation:** While federal mandates have been rolled back, state-level regulations vary widely, with some states maintaining stricter standards.

---

## 5. Technology & AI Innovation

AI and technology adoption are accelerating across the LTC sector:

### Key Applications
- **AI as top priority:** A January 2025 survey found AI tools surpassed EHR usability as the #1 technology priority for healthcare practice leaders (32% ranking it first, up from 13% in late 2023).
- **Fall detection & prevention:** AI-powered systems like SeniorLife.AI have demonstrated a 49% improvement in balance and 58% reduction in falls in studies involving 1,200+ seniors.
- **Predictive analytics:** Machine learning algorithms analyze patient data to predict health deterioration before it becomes severe.
- **Administrative automation:** AI streamlines documentation, care coordination, and caregiver scheduling optimization.
- **Smart home technology:** Voice-activated assistants, automated lighting, motion sensors, and wearable health monitors enable safer aging in place.

### Cutting-Edge Developments
- **Physical AI care systems:** Avadin™, launched at CES 2026, integrates AI robotics, digital twin intelligence, VR/AR cognitive programs, and clinician-facing apps into a unified care platform.
- **Agentic AI:** Researchers are exploring autonomous AI caregivers for personalized interactions, health monitoring, and cognitive support.
- **Eldercare robotics market:** Projected to grow at a CAGR of ~14.8% through 2030.

### Challenges
- **Digital divide:** Older adults without access to technology risk being left behind, amplifying existing health disparities.
- **Ethics & privacy:** Balancing innovation with safeguards for elderly healthcare data remains critical.
- **Human oversight:** AI outputs require professional review — it augments but does not replace human caregiving.

---

## 6. Long-Term Care Insurance Market

### Market Overview
- The LTCI market is estimated at $176.6 billion in 2025, projected to reach $310.9 billion by 2035 (CAGR of 5.82%).
- Over **6.9 million Americans** currently hold LTC insurance policies; insurers paid out $16.8 billion in 2024.

### Key Trends
- **Declining consumer interest:** 40% of Americans 29+ say they do not plan to buy coverage (up from 32% the prior year), even though ~70% of those turning 65 will need LTC.
- **Rising premiums:** Insurers continue raising premiums on existing policies due to higher-than-expected claims and rising care costs.
- **Shift to hybrid products:** Combination life/LTC insurance products are expanding, particularly targeting millennials in the "sandwich generation." These products make coverage accessible to younger, middle-income consumers.
- **Major reinsurance activity:** Unum Group announced a $3.4 billion LTC reinsurance deal in February 2025, the third major deal in recent years.
- **New product innovation:** CareScout Insurance Company (a Genworth affiliate) launched a new standalone LTCI product with flexible benefit pools and inflation protection.

### Legislative Developments
- **Washington State (WA Cares Fund):** SB 5291 enacted in May 2025 introduced supplemental LTC insurance standards, opt-out rescission options, and automatic inflation protection.
- **Federal WISH Act:** Reintroduced in March 2025, this bipartisan bill would create a federally administered LTC insurance trust fund for at-home care benefits.

---

## 7. Evolving Consumer Expectations & Care Models

- **Lifestyle-oriented communities:** Demand is growing for resort-style senior living with wellness amenities, social engagement, and modern design. Today's senior consumer seeks a lifestyle product, not just a care product.
- **Specialization:** Providers are expanding beyond basic custodial care into memory care, palliative/hospice care, rehabilitation, and disease-specific programs.
- **Value-based care:** Institutional Special Needs Plan (I-SNP) enrollment grew from 50,000 (2015) to 130,000 (2025), creating value-based revenue streams beyond traditional fee-for-service.
- **Person-centered care:** Home care agencies are designing individualized plans reflecting medical needs, personal preferences, lifestyle goals, and emotional wellness — including music programs, art therapy, and companionship.
- **Family caregiver support:** Growing emphasis on education, counseling, respite care, financial assistance, and digital tools (scheduling apps, virtual support groups) for family caregivers.

---

## 8. Industry Outlook

### Challenges
- Persistent workforce shortages, especially in rural areas
- Regulatory uncertainty at the federal level
- Rising costs and funding pressures (Medicaid reimbursement gaps)
- Digital divide and equity concerns with technology adoption
- Declining LTC insurance coverage leaving families financially exposed

### Opportunities
- Rapid growth in home-based and community-based services
- AI and technology-driven care transformation
- Hybrid insurance products expanding coverage to younger demographics
- Value-based care models improving outcomes and revenue
- Growing consumer demand for personalized, lifestyle-oriented care
- State-level policy innovation (Medicaid waivers, caregiver support programs)

### Bottom Line
The LTC industry in 2026 faces real headwinds — but the scale of the demographic wave and the pace of innovation present enormous opportunities for providers willing to adapt. Success will depend on workforce strategy, technology adoption, diversified care models, and agility in navigating an evolving regulatory environment.
